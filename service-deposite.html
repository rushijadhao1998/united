United Rural - Types of Deposite

At United Rural Credit Co-Op. Society Ltd., We offer a range of deposit accounts tailored to meet the needs of individuals, businesses, and communities in rural areas. Whether you are saving for the future, managing daily expenses, or growing your business, we have the right deposit account for you.

  • Saving Account

A savings account is a basic bank or credit union account designed to securely store your money while allowing you to earn interest on your deposits and providing easy access to your funds when needed. It's ideal for saving for short-to-medium-term financial goals, such as an emergency fund or a down payment, by offering a safe, liquid, and interest-bearing place for your money. You can Earn 4.00% p.a. interest in Saving Account with United Rural Society.

  • Recurring Deposite

A recurring deposit is a special kind of term deposit in India that is offered by Indian banks and India Post, which helps people with regular incomes to deposit a fixed amount every month into their recurring deposit account and earn interest at the rate applicable to fixed deposits. You can Earn Up To 10.50% p.a. interest in Recurring Deposite (RD) with United Rural Society.

  • Fixed Deposite

A fixed deposit (FD) is a financial instrument that offers guaranteed returns on a lump-sum investment for a specific period. It is considered a secure, low-risk option, and the interest rate is fixed at the time of deposit. At the end of the term, or "maturity," you receive the initial amount plus the accumulated interest. You can Earn Up To 10.00% p.a. interest in Fixed Deposite (FD) with United Rural Society.

  • Current Account

A current bank account is designed for frequent daily transactions, primarily used by businesses, entrepreneurs, and professionals. Key features include high transaction limits, the ability to make unlimited deposits and withdrawals without notice, and typically no interest paid on the balance. Businesses also benefit from overdraft facilities and specialized services like trade finance.

  • Dam Duppat

"Dam Duppat" refers to a type of fixed deposit scheme, primarily offered by banks and cooperative societies in India, particularly in the state of Maharashtra. The term itself is a Hindi-Marathi phrase meaning "double money". Under this plan, an initial lump-sum investment doubles in value after a predetermined period. Let's Make your Money Double in 8 Years with United Rural Society.

  • Monthly Income Scheme (MIS)

A monthly income scheme (MIS) provides a fixed stream of monthly income from a lump-sum investment, often in government-backed options like the Post Office Monthly Income Scheme (POMIS) or bank-offered products. These low-risk, long-term investment options are ideal for risk-averse individuals and retirees who need a regular income supplement. Key features include a fixed maturity period (typically 5 years), eligibility for individuals and joint accounts, nomination facilities, and the ability to transfer accounts between post offices or banks. You can Earn Up To 9.60% p.a. interest in Monthly Income Scheme (MIS) with United Rural Society.
